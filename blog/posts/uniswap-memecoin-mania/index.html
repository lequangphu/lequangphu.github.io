Context

As of May 20, 2023, there are 706 meme coins listed on Coinmarketcap.

What is a meme coin?

  • A meme coin is a cryptocurrency that doesn’t take itself as seriously as leading digital currencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum. Dogecoin (DOGE) - widely regarded as the first meme cryptocurrency.

  • A meme coin doesn’t need to have a purpose that is as closely tied to their underlying value, like Bitcoin and Ethereum. A meme coin often seems to have the sole purpose of “going to the moon,” a phrase popularized by Dogecoin’s skyrocketing price in early 2021.

Started on Apr 14, interest in meme coins surged, led by PEPE, TURBO, and WOJAK. The meme coin mania has pushed Uniswap’ trading volume above Coinbase’s trading volume in recent days.

In this analysis

I’ll answer a few questions below:

  • Did the increase in popularity of Memecoins attract new users to the Uniswap platform?

  • Who are the participants actively engaging with Memecoins on Uniswap? How are the whales behaving, do they have a big impact on the market?

  • Has the introduction of Memecoins led to an increase or decrease in the number of pairs created on Uniswap? Are there any notable trends or preferences in terms of the Memecoin pairs being created?

  • Did the rise of Memecoins have any discernible effect on the activity and liquidity of “normal” pairs on Uniswap? Are there any correlations or shifts in trading behavior?

Method

PEPE, TURBO, and WOJAK represent all of meme coins on Ethereum.

Key Highlights

The Rise of Meme

On Ethereum, at its peak, meme pools acounted for 1/3 in total trading volume.

And their share is a bit higher at 37% in total swapper.

While only accounted for up to 1/5 in total transaction. Which means those who joined the mania were more aggressive in trading size than those who didn’t.

How much of those happened on Uniswap?

The mania happened on 6 Dexs, and only 1% of trading activities didn’t happen on Uniswap as seen in the bubble chart below. The pink buble is so much bigger, and so far away from the rest.

So the mania on Ethereum was basically the mania on Uniswap. Let’s look into its effects on Uniswap.

Effects On Uniswap

New Swapper

The rise in total new swapper was highly correlated to the rise of % share of meme pools in total new swapper. At its peak, meme pools accounted for almost half of total new swapper.

In a few days that trading activities of meme coins surged, % share of new swapper in meme pools rose higher than that in other pools, the difference can be as high as 1.5x (20% vs. 14%).

Thus, it is safe to say that the mania did attract new swapper to Uniswap.

However, it raised another question, which is 👇

How sticky are these meme swappers?

Retention Rate
% of a cohort return (to swap on Uniswap).

Retention rate of meme swappers in the first 3 weeks is 2x of Uniswap average. However, from week 4th, the trend appears to drop faster than Uniswap average.
