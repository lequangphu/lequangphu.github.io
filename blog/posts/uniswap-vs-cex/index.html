In this post, I will compare crypto trading activities on Uniswap to those on Centralized Exchanges (CEXs). But first, let’s have a quick lookback on some of key events in crypto exchange history.

On Jan 3, 2009, Satoshi Nakamoto mined the first block of Bitcoin.

One year later, on Mar 17, 2010, Bitcoinmarket.com, the first crypto exchange went live. (ref)

Four months later, on Jul 18, 2010, mtgox.com, the famous defunct exchange, launched. By Apr 2013, and into early 2014, it became the largest Bitcoin exchange, handling over 70% of total Bitcoin trades. On Feb 28, 2014, after a series of major security breaches, and bitcoin stolens, the exchange filed for bankruptcy. (ref)

Meanwhile, in Jun 2012, Brian Amstrong founded Coinbase, the current largest CEX in the US. (ref)

Five years after the launch of Coinbase, on Jul 14, 2017, Changpeng Zhao founded Binance, the current largest CEX in the world. (ref)

Over a year later, on Nov 2, 2018, Hayden Adams founded Uniswap, the first, and also the current largest DEX in the world. (ref)

It’s been 14 years since the Bitcoin’s genesis block mined, 13 years since the first CEX founded, and almost 4 years since the first DEX founded. With 8 years lesser of operation, how has DEX performed vs. CEX? And with 1 years, and 6 years lesser of operation, how has Uniswap performed vs. Binance, and Coinbase? Let’s find out!

DEX vs. CEX

To compare DEX to CEX, let’s look at DEX to CEX spot trade volume, which is monthly decentralized exchange volume divided by centralized exchange volume (as a percentage).

In just 4 months, DEX to CEX spot trade volume skyrocketed to 16% in Sep 2020 from 0.56% in May 2020. Sixteen months later, it made a higher high to 17% in Jan 2022. And recently, in May 2023, it made all time high at 22%. That marked DEX spot volume surpassed a fifth of that on CEX.

In absolute $, spot trade volume of DEX and CEX’s all time high were $213b and $4.25t, both recorded in May 2021, which translates to 9% of DEX to CEX spot trade volume.

Last 12 months, though both spot trade volume on DEX and CEX have generally been down, DEX to CEX spot trade volume has been up.
