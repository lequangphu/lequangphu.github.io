How is Uniswap doing compare to top centralized exchanges (CEX)? Let’s answer the question with me through this post.

First, let’s have a quick lookback on some of key events in crypto exchange history.

On Jan 3, 2009, Satoshi Nakamoto mined the first block of Bitcoin.

One year later, on Mar 17, 2010, Bitcoinmarket.com, the first crypto exchange went live. link

Four months later, on Jul 18, 2010, mtgox.com, the famous defunct exchange, launched. By Apr 2013, and into early 2014, it became the largest Bitcoin exchange, handling over 70% of total Bitcoin trades. On Feb 28, 2014, after a series of major security breaches, and bitcoin stolens, the exchange filed for bankruptcy. link

Meanwhile, in Jun 2012, Brian Amstrong founded Coinbase, the current largest CEX in the US. link

Six months later, Bitfinex was founded. link

Five years after the launch of Coinbase, on Jul 14, 2017, Changpeng Zhao founded Binance, the current largest crypto exchange in the world. link

In the same year, OKX was launched. link

Over a year later, on Nov 2, 2018, Hayden Adams founded Uniswap, the first, and also the current largest DEX in the world. link

It’s been 14 years since the Bitcoin’s genesis block mined, 13 years since the first CEX founded, and almost 4 years since the first DEX founded. Before progressing to the end of this post, some data points to take note:

  • DEX is 8 years younger than CEX.

  • Uniswap is 6 years younger than Coinbase, Bitfinex, and 1 years younger than Binance, OKX.

DEX vs. CEX

To service a trade, there must be liquidity. First, let’s examine liquidity.

Liquidity

In this section, let’s look at total asset deposited in DEX (i.e. TVL, value locked), and in CEX (i.e. proof of reserves).

Total DEX assets is roughly 15% of total CEX assets.

source: defillama.com.

There are 839 DEXs tracked on Defillama. Top 20 DEXs, which have TVL range between $80M and $4B (to be input later), account for 60% of total DEX assets. Uniswap is the largest DEX in total assets.

source: defillama.com.

There are 28 CEXs, which have proof of reserves (POR) range between $100K and $60B. Top 3 CEXs account for almost 80% of total CEX assets. Binance is the largest CEX in total assets.

source: defillama.com.

In top 10 exchanges, only 2 of them are DEX, which are Uniswap, and Curve, at 4th and 5th position respectively. Their assets are 7.5% or less of that on Binance.

source: defillama.com.

Trades

In this section, to compare DEX to CEX, let’s look at DEX to CEX spot volume, which is monthly decentralized exchange volume divided by centralized exchange volume (as a percentage).

One and a half year after the first data point in Jan 2019, DEX to CEX spot volume first surpassed 1% in Jun 2020. Four months later, DEX to CEX spot volume skyrocketed to 16% in Sep 2020. Sixteen months later, it made a higher high to 17% in Jan 2022.

And recently, in May 2023, it made all time high at 22%. That marked DEX spot volume surpassed a fifth of that on CEX.

source: theblockcrypto.com.

In absolute $, spot volume of DEX and CEX’s all time high were $213b and $4.25t, both recorded in May 2021, which translates to 9% of DEX to CEX spot volume.

Last 12 months, though both spot volume on DEX and CEX have generally been down, DEX to CEX spot volume (as seen above) has been up, which indicates a slower drop in DEX spot volume.

source: theblockcrypto.com.

source: theblockcrypto.com.

Dominances of Uniswap - the largest DEX, and Binance - the largest CEX, in each category are quite similar (40%-60% market share).

Overall, market share of Binance has been in a steeper upward slope than that of Uniswap.

Last 12 months, market shares of top 2-5 CEXs are more evenly distributed than those of top 2-5 DEXs.

source: theblockcrypto.com.

source: theblockcrypto.com.

Chain Breakdown

All Uniswap assets are on EVM compatible chains (EVM). Of $100 worth of assets, almost $90 on Ethereum, $7 on Arbitrum, $2 on Polygon, $1 or less on each of Optimism, BNB, and Celo.

source: defillama.com.

Meanwhile, Binance assets are more evenly distributed among 4 chains: Bitcoin, Ethereum, Tron, and BNB, which account for roughly 90% of total assets. The other 10% is allocated among 10 other chains. On OKX and Bitfinex, assets on Bitcoin and Ethereum dominate heavily, account between 95% and 98% in total assets.

source: defillama.com.

Token Breakdown

Uniswap assets allocate in more than 2k tokens. Of $100 worth of assets, $80 in top 9 tokens, of which almost $40 in WETH, $25 in stablecoins (USDC, USDT, DAI, and FRAX), almost $4 in WBTC.

source: defillama.com.

Mean while, CEX assets are less diversified in number of tokens (less than 100). On Binance, of $100 worth of assets, about $60 roughly equally shared between BTC and USDT, $14 in BNB, $11 in WETH. On OKX, of $100 worth of assets, $96 in top 3 tokens: USDT, BTC, and WETH. Similarly, on Bitfinex, of $100 worth of assets, $97 in top 3 tokens: BTC, WETH, and LEO. If you haven’t heard of LEO, you are not alone 🤣.

source: defillama.com.

H1 2023

In the first half of 2023, Uniswap total assets started with $3.2B, and ended with $4B, or 25% increase. It ranged between $2.8B and $4.4B, or max 12% down, and 38% up (peak, trough vs. start).

Meanwhile, on CEXs:

  • Binance total assets started with $54B, and ended with $58B, or 7% increase. It ranged between $54B and $71B, i.e the start is the trough, and max 31% up.

  • OKX total assets started with $6.4B, and ended with $10.3B, or 60% increase. The start is also the trough, and the end is also roughly the peak. These imply a strong uptrend.

  • Bitfinex total assets started with $6.3B, and ended with $9.9B, or 57% increase. The trend is similar to that of OKX.

source: defillama.com.
