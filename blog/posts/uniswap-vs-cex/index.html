In this post, I will compare crypto trading activities on Uniswap to those on Centralized Exchanges (CEXs). But first, let’s have a quick lookback on some of key events in crypto exchange history.

On Jan 3, 2009, Satoshi Nakamoto mined the first block of Bitcoin.

One year later, on Mar 17, 2010, Bitcoinmarket.com, the first crypto exchange went live. link

Four months later, on Jul 18, 2010, mtgox.com, the famous defunct exchange, launched. By Apr 2013, and into early 2014, it became the largest Bitcoin exchange, handling over 70% of total Bitcoin trades. On Feb 28, 2014, after a series of major security breaches, and bitcoin stolens, the exchange filed for bankruptcy. link

Meanwhile, in Jun 2012, Brian Amstrong founded Coinbase, the current largest CEX in the US. link

Five years after the launch of Coinbase, on Jul 14, 2017, Changpeng Zhao founded Binance, the current largest CEX in the world. link

Over a year later, on Nov 2, 2018, Hayden Adams founded Uniswap, the first, and also the current largest DEX in the world. link

It’s been 14 years since the Bitcoin’s genesis block mined, 13 years since the first CEX founded, and almost 4 years since the first DEX founded. With 8 years lesser of operation, how has DEX performed vs. CEX? And with 1 years, and 6 years lesser of operation, how has Uniswap performed vs. Binance, and Coinbase? Let’s find out!

DEX vs. CEX

Trades

In this section, to compare DEX to CEX, let’s look at DEX to CEX spot volume, which is monthly decentralized exchange volume divided by centralized exchange volume (as a percentage).

In just 4 months, DEX to CEX spot volume skyrocketed to 16% in Sep 2020 from 0.56% in May 2020. Sixteen months later, it made a higher high to 17% in Jan 2022. To see this on the chart below, click zoom all at the bottom right corner.

And recently, in May 2023, it made all time high at 22%. That marked DEX spot volume surpassed a fifth of that on CEX.

In absolute $, spot volume of DEX and CEX’s all time high were $213b and $4.25t, both recorded in May 2021, which translates to 9% of DEX to CEX spot volume.

Last 12 months, though both spot volume on DEX and CEX have generally been down, DEX to CEX spot volume (as seen above) has been up, which indicates a slower drop in DEX spot volume.

Liquidity

In this section, to compare DEX to CEX, let’s look at total asset deposited in DEX (i.e. TVL, value locked), and in CEX (i.e. proof of reserves).

In Defi, in term of value locked by category, DEX is the largest one. And lending follows closely behind.

Uniswap is the largest DEX in spot volume, and also the largest DEX in value locked. Note that the chart below shows different versions of Uniswap separately, which is why Curve is at the top of the chart.

Coingecko tracks proof of reserves (POR) of 20 CEXs. Binance has the largest POR, more than 6 times higher than OKX, at no.2.

With $65B POR, total asset of Binance is roughly 17 times higher than that of Uniswap.
