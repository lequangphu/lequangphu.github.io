Crypto Winter Usage of Uniswap

uniswap
crypto
How has Uniswap’s Usage been this Crypto Winter?
Author

Lê Quang Phú

Published

September 27, 2023

1 Introduction

Uniswap launched on November 2, 2018. In July 2021, Uniswap started expanding to other chains. At the end of August 2023, on the trading side, Uniswap had 11.9m in total traders, who traded $1.6t in total volume. On the providing liquidity side, Uniswap had 1.2m in total liquidity providers, who added and removed liquidity, resulting in a net liquidity of $3.8b.

In this post, you will learn about Uniswap’s usage, of both trading and providing liquidity, on a monthly basis from May 2021, when Uniswap’s total monthly volume reached its all-time high, to August 2023, i.e. this crypto winter. Then I’ll dive deeper into Uniswap’s usage on Ethereum, the first and largest chain of Uniswap, both on a monthly basis and at the end of August 2023 snapshot.

2 Key takeaways

  1. On a monthly basis, from May 2021, when Uniswap monthly volume reached its all-time high, to August 2023, both Uniswap’s monthly volume and end-of-month TVL dropped more than 50% from all-time highs. Meanwhile, monthly active traders and monthly active LPs increased significantly, 80% or more versus the numbers in May 2021, thanks to the expansions to other chains, started in July 2021.

  2. On a monthly basis, from May 2021 to August 2023, on Ethereum-based Uniswap:

  • About trading:
    • New traders contributed more than return traders in the recovery of number of traders after June 2022.
    • Uniswap v2 was the main driver behind the fall and rise of number of new trader.
    • M-1 return traders, who returned after being active in the previous month, was the main driver behind the decrease, before June 2022, and the recovery of number of return trader, after June 2022. Interestingly, number of M-7 or longer return traders, who returned after being dormant 7 months or longer, was also increasing, contributed to the recovery of return traders.
  • About providing liquidity:
    • Before June 2022, total monthly active LPs were down, number of return LPs was larger than that of new LPs. After June 2022, total monthly active LPs started to recover, number of return LPs was smaller than that of new LPs. During the whole period, number of return LPs dropped faster on the way down, and recovered slower on the way up than that of new LPs.
    • Uniswap v2 was the main driver behind the fall and rise of number of new LPs.
    • M-1 return LPs, who returned after being active in the previous month, was the main driver behind the fall and rise of number of return LPs.
  1. At the end of August 2023, during 12 months started in September 2022, on Ethereum-based Uniswap:
  • About trading:
    • There were more dormant traders than active traders.
    • If traders were active in August 2023, 23% of them were still active on other DEXs (including DEXs on Ethreum and other chains). That number increases to 30% if they were dormant in August 2023, drops to 12% if they were dormant for 13-24 months, drops to 6% if they were dormant for more than 24 months.
    • 5 most favorite Ethereum-based DEXs of traders after their last trade on Ethereum-based Uniswap were Sushiswap, 0x API, airswap, curve, shibaswap.
    • 5 most favorite other-chains-based DEXs of traders after their last trade on Ethereum-based Uniswap were pancakeswap, sushiswap, quickswap, camelot, trader_joe.
  • About providing liquidity:
    • There were more dormant LPs than active LPs.
    • There were more LPs with at least one open position than LPs with no open position.
    • There were more LPs, who never closed any position than LPs, who closed at least one position.
    • The longer LPs being dormant, the less likely that they gained from providing liquidity. If LPs were dormant more than 12 months, the longer being dormant, the more likely that they had lost from providing liquidity.

3 Uniswap, monthly

This section has 4 sub-sections. The first 2 sub-sections are about trading and providing liquidity. The third sub-section tried to answer the question: what would have happened if Uniswap had not expanded to other chains? The last sub-section summarizes the first three sub-sections.

3.1 Trading

3.1.1 Volume

  • In May 2021, Uniswap’s total monthly volume (the number) reached its all-time high, reaching $90.8b. Since July 2021, when Uniswap started expanding to other chains, Ethereum-based Uniswap’s share in the number reached its all-time low in April 2023, reaching 63.5%.
  • In August 2023, the number was $25.9b, reflecting a 71% drop from the all-time high. Meanwhile, Ethereum-based Uniswap’s share in the number was 76.1%, a 12.6% increase from the all-time low.

3.1.2 Traders

  • Uniswap’s total number of traders (the number) reached its all-time high in May 2023, reaching 1.8m, a 80% increase from May 2021. Meanwhile, Ethereum-based Uniswap’s share in the number decreased rapidly, reached its all-time low in June 2023, reaching 32.2%.
  • In August 2023, the number was 1.4m, reflecting a 22% drop from the all-time high. Meanwhile, Ethereum-based Uniswap’s share in the number was 38.8%, a 6.6% increase from the all-time low.

3.2 Providing liquidity

3.2.1 TVL

Monthly TVL is calculated at end of month.

  • Uniswap’s monthly TVL (the number) reached its all-time high in November 2021, reaching $8.7b. Since then, it gradually decreased to $3.8b in December 2022. Since then, it ranged between $3.8b and $4.9b. Meanwhile, Ethereum-based Uniswap’s share in the number was declining, but has never dropped below 89%.
  • At the end of August 2023, the number was $3.8b, reflecting a 56% drop from the all-time high. Meanwhile, Ethereum-based Uniswap’s share in the number was 91.2%.

3.2.2 LPs

  • The trends of Uniswap’s total monthly active LPs (the number) and Ethereum-based Uniswap’s share in the number were quite opposite to each other. In April 2023, the number reached its all-time high, reaching 119.5k, a 85% increase from May 2021. And Ethereum-based Uniswap’s share in the number reached its all-time low, reaching 37.1%.
  • In August 2023, the number was 74k, reflecting a 38% drop from the all-time high. Meanwhile, Ethereum-based Uniswap’s share in the number was 56.1%, a 19% increase from the all-time low.

3.3 What if Uniswap had not expanded to other chains?

If Uniswap had not expanded to other chains, in 2023:

  • total monthly volume would have been 16%-37% lower,
  • total monthly TVL would have been 6%-10% lower,
  • total monthly active traders would have been 43%-68% lower,
  • total monthly active LPs would have been 42%-63% lower,

In short, the impact of Uniswap not expanding to other chains would have been non-trivial, especially on total monthly active traders and LPs.

3.4 Summary

From May 2021 to August 2023, both monthly volume and TVL dropped significantly from the all-time highs, 71% and 56% respectively. Meanwhile, monthly active traders and monthly active LPs grew significantly, thanks to the expansions to other chains. Without the expansions, they would have been declining, not growing.

In the next sections, let’s dive deeper into Uniswap usage on Ethereum.

4 Ethereum-based Uniswap, monthly

This section has three sub-sections. The first sub-section is about trading. The second sub-section is about providing liquidity. The last sub-section summarizes the first two sub-sections.

4.1 Trading

4.1.1 New and return in total

After May 2021:

  • In absolute number of change, Uniswap’s total monthly volume dropped sharply 2 months after its all-time high, mostly in volume of return traders, which has never accounted for less than 71%. In rate of change, total monthly volume of both new and return traders were down at similar rates. However, in 2023, total monthly volume of new traders dropped slower than that of return traders, reflected in downward slope of the percentage of volume of return traders in total monthly volume.
  • Uniswap’s monthly active new traders has never dropped below 100k. It bottomed at 101.3k in June 2022. It usually lead 1 month the trend of monthly active return traders. The latter bottomed at 224k in July 2022. Since then, the two trends were generally up. However, number of monthly active new traders were increasing faster than number of monthly active return traders, reflected in downward slope of the percentage of return traders in total monthly active traders.
  • Both monthly volume per trader of new and return traders were down generally. Monthly volume per return trader was usually 4x-10x larger than that of new trader. In 2023, though monthly volume per trader of both groups were down, the gap was smaller than in the prior years, which indicates that monthly volume per return traders dropped faster than that of new traders.

4.1.2 New trader

  • Uniswap’s total monthly active new traders correlated the most with Uniswap v2’s new traders, as the latter accounted for at least 51% of the former monthly.

4.1.3 Return trader

  • Uniswap’s total monthly active return traders correlated the most with M-1 return traders, who returned after trading in the previous month. Except for January 2023, M-1 return traders has never accounted for less than half of total monthly active return traders.
  • Interestingly, number of M-7 or longer return traders, who returned after being dormant for 7 months or longer, had been increasing and peaked in May 2023.
  • The main driver of the rise of M-7 or longer return were a subset of return traders, who returned after being dormant for 7-24 months, which consist of 2 smaller subsets: M-7 - M-12 and M-13 - M-24. Interestingly, the latter were smaller than the former in all months except for May 2023, when the latter peaked at 9.9% while the former were 7.7%.

4.2 Providing liquidity

This section has three sub-sections. The first sub-section is about contribution of new and return in total LPs. The last two sub-sections dive deeper into new and return LPs.

4.2.1 New and return in total

After May 2021:

  • Both monthly number of new and return LPs had been down until June 2022. Since then, both numbers were up.
  • Meanwhile, the percentage of return LPs in total monthly active LPs kept decreasing during the whole period. In other words, number of return LPs dropped faster on the way down, and recovered slower on the way up than that of new LPs.

4.2.2 New LP

  • Uniswap’s total monthly active new LPs correlated the most with Uniswap v2’s new LPs, as the latter accounted for at least 58% of the former monthly.

4.2.3 Return LP

  • Uniswap’s total monthly active return LPs correlated the most with M-1 return LPs, who returned after trading in the previous month. M-1 return LPs is the largest subset, accounted for at least 44% of total monthly active return LPs.
  • Similar to but not as clear as number of M-7 or longer return traders, number of M-7 or longer return LPs, who returned after being dormant for 7 months or longer, had been increasing and peaked in February 2023.
  • The group of M-7 - M-12 return LPs was the driver of the rise of M-7 or longer return LPs in 2022. Recently, number of M-25 or longer return LPs was increasing.

4.3 Summary

From May 2021 to August 2023, Ethereum-based Uniswap monthly performance:

  • Total monthly volume and total monthly active traders were generally down. Before June 2022, volume dropped faster than number of trader. After June 2022, volume kept dropping, but slower, while number of traders started to recover. New traders contributed more than return traders in the recovery of number of trader. Uniswap v2 was the main driver behind the fall and rise of number of new trader. Meanwhile, M-1 return traders, who returned after being active in the previous month, was the main driver behind the fall and rise of number of return trader. While other subsets of return traders fell then rose, M-7 or longer return traders, who returned after being dormant for 7 months or longer, kept rising during most of the period.
  • Before June 2022, total monthly active LPs were down, number of return LPs was larger than that of new LPs. After June 2022, total monthly active LPs started to recover, number of return LPs was smaller than that of new LPs. During the whole period, number of return LPs dropped faster on the way down, and recovered slower on the way up than that of new LPs. Similar to the trading side, Uniswap v2 was the main driver behind the fall and rise of number of new LPs. Meanwhile M-1 return LPs, who returned after being active in the previous month, was the main driver behind the fall and rise of number of return LPs.

5 Ethereum-based Uniswap’s traders, August 2023 snapshot

This section has 4 sub-sections. The first sub-section is about all traders. The next 2 sub-sections are about traders who were active and dormant between September 2022 and August 2023. The last sub-section summarizes the first 3 sub-sections.

5.1 All traders

From November 2018, when Uniswap launched, to August 2023:

  • Most of Uniswap’s traders have their last trade after August 2020.
  • To at least 45% of LPs, who have their last trade in a given month, that last month is also their first month. August 2023 is excluded in this number as it is the last observed month.

At the end of August 2023, 7.5m traders had traded on Uniswap on Ethereum, out of every 100 traders:

  • 59 traders were dormant, i.e. did not trade, between September 2022 and August 2023, i.e. Dormant 12 months traders,
  • 41 traders were active, i.e. traded at least once, between September 2022 and August 2023, i.e. Active 12 months traders.

Next, let’s dive deeper into 2 groups of traders above.

5.2 Traders, active between September 2022 and August 2023

3.1m traders were active between September 2022 and August 2023, out of every 100 traders:

  • 82 traders were dormant in August 2023,
  • 18 traders were active in August 2023.

5.2.1 What percentage of each group active elsewhere?

Traders who were active on Uniswap between September 2022 and August 2023:

  • if they were dormant in August 2023:
    • 3 out of 10 were active on other DEXs,
    • 13 out of 100 were active on Uniswap on other chains.
  • if they were active in August 2023:
    • 23 out of 100 were active on other DEXs,
    • 1 out of 10 were active on Uniswap on other chains.

5.2.2 Where was elsewhere, exactly?

The traders, who had their last trade on Uniswap on Ethereum between September 2022 and August 2023, then traded on other DEXs:

  • on Ethereum:
    • 83% of them had their last trade on sushiswap, 0x API, airswap, curve, shibaswap,
    • the rest had their last trade on 16 other DEXs.
  • on other chains:
    • 78% of them had their last trade on pancakeswap, sushiswap, quickswap, camelot, trader_joe,
    • the rest had their last trade on 37 other DEXs.

5.3 Traders, dormant between September 2022 and August 2023

4.5m traders were dormant between September 2022 and August 2023, out of every 100 traders:

  • 51 traders were dormant 13-24 months,
  • 49 traders were dormant more than 24 months,

5.3.1 What percentage of each group active elsewhere?

Traders, who had their last trade on Uniswap before September 2022, but traded on other DEXs after September 2022:

  • if they were dormant for 13-24 months:
    • 12 out of 100 were active on other DEXs,
    • 4 out of 100 were active on Uniswap on other chains.
  • if they were dormant for more than 24 months:
    • 6 out of 100 were active on other DEXs,
    • 2 out of 100 were active on Uniswap on other chains.

5.3.2 Where was elsewhere, exactly?

Traders, who had their last trade on Uniswap before September 2022, and traded on other DEXs after September 2022:

  • on Ethereum:
    • 88% of them had their last trade on sushiswap, airswap, 0x API, shibaswap, curve,
    • the rest had their last trade on 16 other DEXs.
  • on other chains:
    • 81% had their last trade on pancakeswap, quickswap, sushiswap, camelot, trader_joe,
    • the rest had their last trade on 36 other DEXs.

5.4 Summary

At the end of August 2023, Ethereum-based Uniswap’s traders:

  • Most of Ethereum-based Uniswap’s traders have their last trade after August 2020. At least 45% of traders have the last trade in the same month with the first trade.
  • There were more dormant traders than active traders during 12 months started in September 2022.
  • Out of every 10 traders, who were active in the 12 months period, 8 traders were dormant in August 2023, and 2 traders were active in August 2023. If traders were were active in August 2023, 23% of them were still active on other DEXs. That number increases to 30% if they were dormant in August 2023.
  • Out of every 10 traders, who were dormant in the 12 months period, 5 traders were dormant for 13-24 months, 4 traders were dormant for more than 24 months. If traders were dormant for 13-24 months, 12% of them were still active on other DEXs. That number drops to 6% if they were dormant for more than 24 months.
  • 5 most favorite Ethereum-based DEXs of traders after their last trade on Ethereum-based Uniswap were Sushiswap, 0x API, airswap, curve, shibaswap.
  • 5 most favorite other-chains-based DEXs of traders after their last trade on Ethereum-based Uniswap were pancakeswap, sushiswap, quickswap, camelot, trader_joe.

6 Ethereum-based Uniswap’s LPs, August 2023 snapshot

This section has 4 sub-sections. The first sub-section is about all LPs. The next 2 sub-sections are about LPs who were active and dormant between September 2022 and August 2023. The last sub-section summarizes the first 3 sub-sections.

6.1 All LPs

From November 2018, when Uniswap launched, to August 2023:

  • Most of LPs of Uniswap have their last activity after August 2020.
  • To at least 1/3 of LPs, who have their last activity in a given month, that last month is also their first month.
  • 40.9%-72.9% of LPs, who have their last activity in a given month, still have at least 1 open liquidity position at the end of August 2023.

At the end of August 2023, 927k LPs had provided liquidity on Uniswap on Ethereum:

  • 56% of LPs were dormant, i.e. did not add or remove liquidity, and 44% of LPs were active, i.e. added or removed liquidity at least once, between September 2022 and August 2023.
  • 61% of LPs have at least 1 open position, and 39% of LPs have no open position.
  • When combining the 2 charts above and dive deeper, at the end of August 2023:
    • 63% of 409.5k LPs, who were active in 12 months started in September 2022, have at least 1 open liquidity position.
    • 60% of 517.9k LPs, who were dormant in 12 months started in September 2022, have at least 1 open liquidity position.

6.2 LPs, active between September 2022 and August 2023

410k LPs were active between September 2022 and August 2023, out of every 100 LPs:

  • 79 LPs were dormant in August 2023,
  • 21 LPs were active in August 2023.

6.3 LPs, dormant between September 2022 and August 2023

518k LPs were dormant between September 2022 and August 2023, out of every 100 LPs:

  • 57 LPs were dormant for more than 24 months
  • 43 LPs were dormant 13-24 months.

6.4 Gain, loss from providing liquidity

At the end of August 2023, on all 3 versions of Ethereum-based Uniswap, out of every 100 LPs:

  • 51 LPs never completely removed (closed) any position.
  • 49 LPs have closed at least 1 position.
  • Out of every 10 LPs, who have closed at least 1 position, 4 LPs have price data to calculate their PnL,
  • Out of every 100 LPs who have PnL calculated, 65 LPs gained, 25 LPs lost, 10 LPs were break even from providing liquidity,
  • When diving deeper into these LPs, 2 patterns emerge:
    • The percentage of LPs who had gained from providing liquidity of a subset decreases as the longer LPs of the subset being dormant. In other words, the longer LPs being dormant, the less likely that they had gained from providing liquidity.
    • In subsets which LPs were dormant more than 12 months, the percentage of LPs who had lost from providing liquidity of a subset increases as the longer LPs of the subset being dormant. In other words, if LPs were dormant more than 12 months, the longer being dormant, the more likely that they had lost from providing liquidity.

6.5 Summary

At the end of August 2023, Ethereum-based Uniswap’s LPs:

  • Most of Ethereum-based Uniswap’s LPs have their last activity after August 2020. At least 34% of LPs have the last activity in the same month with the first activity.
  • There were more dormant LPs than active LPs during 12 months started in September 2022.
  • There were more LPs with at least one open position than LPs with no open position.
  • Out of every 100 LPs, who were active in the 12 months period, 79 LPs were dormant in August 2023, and 21 LPs were active in August 2023.
  • Out of every 100 LPs, who were dormant in the 12 months period, 57 LPs were dormant for more than 24 months, 43 LPs were dormant for 13-24 months.
  • There were more LPs, who never closed any position than LPs, who closed at least one position.
  • Out of every 100 LPs, who closed at least one position, 65 LPs gained, 25 LPs lost, 10 LPs were break even.
  • The longer LPs being dormant, the less likely that they gained from providing liquidity.
  • If LPs were dormant more than 12 months, the longer being dormant, the more likely that they had lost from providing liquidity.
